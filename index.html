What is Code Against the Machine...

Often times performing artists don't have access to the resources, funds, or support to hack on their own technologies for live performances. The purpose of this beginner-friendly workshop is to introduce performing artists to different ways of building interactive systems using more accesible technologies.

Intro to programming, electronics, etc

No experience is required to participate in this workshop, in fact, the less experience you have with programming and electronics the better! Workshop participants will learn to program a small device called the micro:bit which is a great tool for learning to prototype technologies, work with sensors, and control some kind of artistic output.

About the facilitator

Annie Kelly is a PhD student at CU Boulder's ATLAS Institute. She also plays bass guitar and sings in a rock band. She is interesting in how we can empower more artistic women to learn to work with technology.

How to sign-up

Space is limited in this workshop so please fill out an interest form here. You must be 18+ to sign-up and we will be giving priority to women and non-binary artists. If you are a man please consider inviting a woman to attend with you and note this on your form. Another thing to note that this workshop is also part of a research study (Annie is a PhD student afterall) and at the start of the workshop we will be asking participants if they are willing to consent to being audio and video recorded.
